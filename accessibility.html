Accessibility

Built for everyone who needs to go.

Restroom access is not just a disability issue — it is an age issue, a parenting issue, a health issue, and a dignity issue. PortaDash is built to serve every person who has ever felt anxious, stranded, or excluded because they could not find the right restroom in time.

Who we serve

Access matters at every age and ability.

The need for a clean, accessible, and nearby restroom is universal — but the urgency is not equal. Some people face it every single day.

Aging Adults

Older adults experience greater urgency, reduced mobility, and a higher need for grab bars and accessible stalls. Finding the right restroom fast is not a convenience — it is a necessity for staying active and independent.

1 in 6 Americans are 65+

Families with Young Children

Toddlers in potty training, infants who need changing, and young children with small bladders turn every outing into a restroom scavenger hunt. Parents need answers in seconds, not minutes.

Over 11 million families with children under 5

People with Chronic Conditions

Crohn's disease, IBD, IBS, diabetes, pregnancy, and dozens of other conditions create urgent and unpredictable restroom needs. These are invisible disabilities that affect millions of people who rarely look disabled.

Over 3 million Americans live with IBD alone

People with Disabilities

Wheelchair users, people with mobility aids, and those who need grab bars, turning radius clearance, or specific stall dimensions cannot simply use any restroom. Every detail on every pin matters.

1 in 4 US adults live with a disability

Our commitment

Beyond compliance. Toward inclusion.

The ADA set a floor — not a ceiling. PortaDash is built on the belief that truly accessible design requires going further: involving people with disabilities in what we build, making data open and actionable, and treating access as a right rather than a feature.

  • Human-centered design — Built around real needs, not minimum requirements.
  • Co-creation — People with disabilities and caregivers help shape what we map and how we show it.
  • Open data — Our restroom gap research is publicly available for researchers, advocates, and policymakers.
  • Whole-life approach — We serve every age, ability, and life stage — not just the most visible needs.
  • Continuous improvement — Accessibility features are a roadmap, not a checkbox.

PortaDash supports the inclusive design principles of the AAPD Access Coalition, which brings together Starbucks, Walmart, CVS Health, and others to move retail spaces beyond ADA compliance toward genuine inclusion — including restroom accessibility standards.
